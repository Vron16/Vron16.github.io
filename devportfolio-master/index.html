Projects

AT&T Troubleshooting ChatBot

This project is the focal point of my work at AT&T. The chatbot uses a combination of natural language processing and keyword reduction to map customers' queries to a a few broad workflows found in AT&T's large internal workflow application. The bot is hosted on AT&T's internal messaging platform and enables customer service agents to simply interact with it from the comfort of their messaging application to solve issues that customers may be having. The bot's backend includes an authentication system that ensures only customer service agents have permission to work with it in addition to access to much of the workflow application that powers nearly all of AT&T's troubleshooting applications.

Gesture Authentication Study Web Application

Over a six month period at the Rutgers Wireless Information and Network Laboratory, I developed a web application capable of allowing users to create gesture-based passwords and then assign scores based on the strength of those passwords. The web application included several forms designed to gather demographic information about the research participants and then many sketchpads that drew paths following the users' touch, permitting them to create patterns based on their touch gestures. At the conclusion of the study, it stored each participants' password in a database and retrieved the passwords as needed for analysis by the professor who commissioned the project.
